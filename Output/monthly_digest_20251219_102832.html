Monthly Digest: MarTech Trends in the Nordics

Executive Summary: The Year of "Agentic" Execution. As we close 2025, the MarTech landscape is pivoting from experimentation to infrastructure. While 2024 was about testing Generative AI, recent data confirms that 2025 became the year AI stopped being optional and became the operational core of marketing (The Drum).

However, we observe a critical "prioritization paradox." While European marketing leaders rank implementing Gen AI as a top-three priority, they simultaneously rank it 17th in overall business importance, suggesting a struggle to prove strategic ROI beyond efficiency gains (Marketing Week).

For the Nordics specifically, the picture is nuanced. Stockholm is cementing its status as a private market hub challenging London and Berlin (PitchBook), yet consumer sentiment in regions like Norway shows skepticism, with only 22% feeling AI impacts their daily lives—drastically lower than the global average (Marketing Week).

Key Strategic Themes

01

From "Gen AI" to "Agentic AI"

The conversation has shifted from content generation to autonomous action. "Agentic advertising"—where AI agents manage discovery, comparison, and decision-making—is moving into the early majority phase.

Nordic Application: Retailers are leading this charge. Nordic fashion retailer Kappahl is utilizing AI agents to assist store associates, achieving a 4.9/5 customer satisfaction score (Microsoft).

Logistics: AI in the Nordics is moving from pilot to powerhouse in logistics, with autonomous agents now managing delivery exceptions in real-time to meet the high expectations of Scandinavian consumers (nShift).

02

The Visibility Crisis

Search behavior is fundamentally changing. In the B2B sector, 94% of buyers used Large Language Models (LLMs) during their buying journey in 2025.

This creates a stark reality: if a brand is not visible in AI-generated answers, it is losing pipeline before it can even be measured (Marketing Week). This trend will force a move from traditional SEO to "Generative Engine Optimization" in 2026.

03

Commerce Media Overtakes TV

In a historic shift for ad spend, global commerce media revenue ($178.2bn) is projected to surpass TV advertising ($171.1bn) in 2025 (ADWEEK).

This aligns with shifting consumer habits, where one-third of social media users now shop directly on social platforms (Marketing Week).

04

Trust as the Nordic Premium

While technology accelerates, the Nordic consumer remains grounded in values.

Sustainability: Scandinavian shoppers are demanding details on sourcing and materials rather than vague green claims (Practical Ecommerce).

Privacy: 77% of global CMOs now view privacy as a performance metric, not just compliance. Trust is becoming the ultimate growth engine as privacy regulations tighten entering 2026 (The Drum).

Implications for Leaders

  • Resolve the Paradox We must help clients bridge the gap between AI enthusiasm and business value. The focus for 2026 must be on "AI-ready data" and infrastructure that scales, rather than isolated experiments (The Drum).
  • Audit "Answer Engine" Presence Brands need to assess how they appear in ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude results. Traditional SERP tracking is no longer sufficient coverage for brand health.
  • Localize for Precision The divergence in AI sentiment (e.g., Norway's low adoption perception vs. Sweden's tech hub growth) suggests a one-size-fits-all Nordic strategy is risky. Marketing communications in Norway may need to focus more on human utility and less on "AI-powered" hype compared to Sweden.
