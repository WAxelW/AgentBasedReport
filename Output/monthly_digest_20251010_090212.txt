October 2025 — Nordic Business Highlights (last 30 days)

Executive Summary
The Nordics are seeing a concentrated build‑out of dealmaking capacity as global banks and local houses compete for talent ahead of an anticipated M&A wave — a structural shift with immediate implications for advisory costs, talent retention and corporate deal readiness (Bloomberg; Reuters). Simultaneously, energy and sustainability themes are front‑of‑mind: clean power milestones are creating both opportunity and political risk for corporates and utilities in Sweden and the region (Forbes).

  Strategic implication: Multinational corporates and PE sponsors should reassess who is pitching for their deals; new entrants can shift execution capacity and pricing dynamics, and companies should run advisor selection processes that test international and local capabilities. (Source: 7)

- Nordic banks are also beefing up M&A capabilities — a two‑track competition
  Summary: Domestic and regional banks (e.g., Danske, Nordea, Pareto and others) have ramped up deal teams in anticipation of a Nordic deal wave, creating a competitive two‑track market of local and international advisors (Bloomberg, Jan 24).
