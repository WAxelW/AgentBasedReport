September 2025 — Sweden & Nordics (last 30 days)

Executive summary
Sweden and the Nordic region ended September with stronger growth signals, active deal-making and a clear pivot to fiscal stimulus in Sweden’s pre‑election budget. Tech resilience, renewed M&A hiring demand and targeted energy/food supply investments are the dominant themes leaders should factor into near‑term strategy. [1][4][5]

Key developments
- Sweden’s government unveiled an expansive 2026 budget (~80–85bn SEK) with tax cuts, defence spending and household measures to jump‑start growth ahead of elections. [1][2]
- Nordic M&A activity remains high; international banks are recruiting aggressively for Nordic deals as announced volumes pass the $100bn+ threshold for the year. [4]
- Swedish tech continues to outperform macro headwinds — the Dealroom/Business Sweden report highlights resilience and continued investment interest. [5][6]
- Strategic private investment in Nordic food and renewables projects (land‑based salmon, renewables platform financing) underscores sector prioritisation for supply‑chain security and green growth. [10][11]

Main body — notable developments (bullets with short summaries)
- Sweden’s 2026 budget: The right‑leaning coalition proposed an ~80bn SEK stimulus package including income tax cuts, electricity tax reliefs, VAT reductions on food and higher defence outlays; some welfare adjustments target means‑testing for larger families. Designed to lift consumption and confidence into the election year. [1][2][3]
- Business incentives: Separate government measures (c.747m USD package) target business competitiveness, signaling welcome short‑term support for firms but a tilt to politically visible consumer relief. Leaders should expect faster household demand but also increased policy scrutiny. [3]
- Macro outlook: OECD and Nordic banks forecast improving but uneven growth; the region benefits from lower interest burdens and disinflation, while geopolitical risks and global demand remain downside risks. Prioritise scenario planning. [7][8]
- Tech sector resilience: State of the Swedish Tech Ecosystem 2025 shows Swedish startups and scaleups maintaining fundraising and innovation momentum despite broader slowdown — attractive to international investors. Expect continued talent competition and selective hiring. [5][6]
- M&A and talent war: Bloomberg reports >$120bn of Nordic deals YTD; Wall Street banks are expanding teams in the Nordics to capture deal flow, raising salary and retention pressure on domestic employers. Prepare for intensified talent competition and possible acquisition opportunities. [4]
- Energy & food investments: Recent financings for renewables platforms and land‑based salmon projects highlight investor appetite for decarbonised energy and supply‑chain resilience — opportunities for corporate partnerships and offtake contracts. [10][11]
- PMIs & market signals: Sweden’s PMI indicates expansion in manufacturing and services, corroborating official budget timing to stimulate demand; markets remain sensitive to global rate expectations. Short‑term demand uplift is likely if stimulus is enacted. [2][9][12]

Strategic implications — what we’d recommend to clients
- Prioritise cash‑flow and demand capture: Use modest, fast‑to‑deploy offers and pricing aligned with expected household tax relief to capture near‑term volume upside (effort: low; impact: medium‑high). [1][3]
- Talent & retention: Reassess compensation and upskilling for finance, M&A, and tech roles — anticipate poaching by international banks and PE (effort: medium; impact: high). [4][5]
- M&A readiness: Update acquisition theses and carve‑out playbooks; elevated deal flow creates buying and divestment windows (effort: medium; impact: high). [4]
- Green partnerships: Fast‑track pilot partnerships in renewables and sustainable food supply chains to access financing and offtake models (effort: medium; impact: medium‑high). [10][11]
- Scenario planning: Build 12‑ and 24‑month scenarios incorporating stimulus, geopolitical shocks and demand variability; link to KPIs for sustainability and profitability. (effort: medium; impact: high). [7][8]
